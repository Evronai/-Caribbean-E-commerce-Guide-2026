Week 1: Plan Your Store

📝 Step 1.1: Define Your Products

Create a spreadsheet with:

  • Product name – Be specific (e.g., "Handmade Cocoa Butter Soap – 200g")
  • Description – What is it? What problem does it solve?
  • Cost price – What you pay to make/buy it
  • Selling price – What customers pay
  • Shipping weight – In kg or lbs
  • Dimensions – For shipping calculations
  • Stock quantity – How many you have
  • Images needed – List what photos you need
💡 Pro tip: Start with 10-20 products. Don't try to list everything at once. Launch with your best sellers.

📋 Step 1.2: Pricing Strategy

Calculate your price using this formula:

Selling Price = (Cost + Shipping + Payment Fees) × (1 + Profit Margin)

Example for a $10 product in Trinidad:

  • Cost: $10
  • Shipping (average): $5
  • Payment fees (3%): $0.45
  • Subtotal: $15.45
  • Profit margin (30%): $4.64
  • Final price: $20.09 → $20.00

📸 Step 1.3: Product Photos

You need at least 3 photos per product:

  • Hero shot: Product alone on white background
  • Lifestyle shot: Product being used
  • Detail shot: Close-up of texture/features
📱 Free tool: Use your phone in natural light near a window. Edit with Canva or Snapseed (free).

🔧 Step 2.2: Setting Up WooCommerce (Recommended)

Follow these exact steps:

  1. Buy hosting – Go to a Caribbean-friendly host like SiteGround, HostGator, or local providers (TSTT in Trinidad, Flow). Cost: $10-30/month.
  2. Install WordPress – Most hosts have 1-click install.
  3. Install WooCommerce plugin – Go to Plugins → Add New → Search "WooCommerce" → Install → Activate.
  4. Run WooCommerce setup wizard – Enter your store location, currency (TTD, JMD, etc.), and business type.
📋 WooCommerce Setup Checklist:
✓ Store address entered correctly
✓ Currency set to your local currency
✓ Tax settings configured
✓ Shipping zones created
✓ Payment methods configured (next step)

⚙️ Step 2.3: Choose Your Theme

  • Go to Appearance → Themes → Add New
  • Search for "Storefront" (free, built by WooCommerce)
  • Install and activate
  • Customize colors, logo, and layout under Appearance → Customize
💡 Need a logo? Use Canva (free) – search for "logo" templates, customize with your business name, download as PNG.

Weeks 4-5: Set Up Payment Gateway

This is the hardest part for Caribbean businesses. International processors often don't work. Follow these steps carefully.

🏦 Step 3.1: Get a Merchant Account

You need a merchant account from your bank to accept credit cards. This takes 3-8 weeks, so start NOW.

📞 Call your bank and ask:
"I want to accept credit card payments online. Can you provide a merchant account? What are the fees? Which payment gateways do you work with?"

Common Caribbean banks that offer merchant accounts:

  • 🇹🇹 Trinidad: Republic Bank, Scotiabank, RBC
  • 🇯🇲 Jamaica: National Commercial Bank, Scotiabank
  • 🇧🇧 Barbados: Barbados National Bank, CIBC
  • 🇩🇴 Dominican Republic: Banco Popular, Scotiabank
  • 🇰🇾 Cayman: Cayman National Bank, Butterfield

💳 Step 3.2: Choose Your Payment Gateway

Once you have a merchant account, you need a gateway to connect your store to the bank.

Gateway Setup Fee Monthly Fee Transaction Fee Works With
First Atlantic Commerce (FAC) $0-500 $0-50 2.5-4% Caribbean banks
Plug n Pay $0 $0-30 2.9% + $0.30 Caribbean region
PayPal $0 $0 2.9% + $0.30 Most countries
Authorize.net $49 $25 2.9% + $0.30 US accounts only
🇺🇸 US LLC Note: You can use Stripe, Shopify Payments, and Square directly. Apply with your US EIN and bank account.

🔌 Step 3.3: Connect Gateway to WooCommerce

For First Atlantic Commerce (example):

  1. Contact FAC for your API credentials (merchant ID, API key, etc.)
  2. In WordPress, go to Plugins → Add New
  3. Search for "First Atlantic Commerce WooCommerce" or use a generic plugin like "WooCommerce Custom Payment Gateway"
  4. Enter your credentials in WooCommerce → Settings → Payments
  5. Set to "Test Mode" first
Test transaction checklist:
✓ Use test card numbers from your gateway
✓ Verify order appears in WooCommerce
✓ Check that email confirmations send
✓ Test a refund process
✓ Try a failed card to see error message

💡 Alternative: Start with PayPal

While customers prefer credit cards, PayPal works in most Caribbean countries and is much faster to set up. Use it as your first payment method while you wait for your merchant account.

Week 6: Add Products & Content

📝 Step 4.1: Add Products to WooCommerce

  1. Go to Products → Add New
  2. Enter product name (e.g., "Trinidad Hot Pepper Sauce – 250ml")
  3. Write description:
    • First paragraph: What it is and key benefit
    • Bullet points: Features, ingredients, size
    • Last paragraph: Why choose your brand
  4. Set price (regular price, sale price if applicable)
  5. Upload product images (featured image + gallery)
  6. Set inventory (stock quantity, SKU)
  7. Set shipping (weight, dimensions)
  8. Set categories (e.g., "Hot Sauces," "Gifts")
  9. Click "Publish"
📋 Example product description:
"Our Trinidad Scorpion Pepper Sauce brings authentic Caribbean heat to your kitchen. Made with fresh scorpion peppers, garlic, and local spices, this small-batch sauce packs serious flavor.

• Heat level: 8/10
• Ingredients: Scorpion peppers, vinegar, garlic, salt
• Size: 250ml glass bottle
• Shelf life: 2 years unopened

Perfect for stews, marinades, or adding kick to your favorite dishes."

📄 Step 4.2: Create Essential Pages

Go to Pages → Add New and create:

  • About Us – Your story, why you started, your values
  • Shipping Policy – Costs, delivery times, carriers
  • Returns Policy – Can customers return? How? Who pays?
  • Privacy Policy – How you handle customer data (required by law)
  • Terms of Service – Legal terms for using your site
  • Contact Us – Email, phone, contact form

⚡ Step 4.3: Set Up Shipping

In WooCommerce → Settings → Shipping:

  1. Add shipping zones (e.g., "Trinidad," "Caribbean," "USA")
  2. For each zone, add shipping methods:
    • Flat rate: Fixed price per order (e.g., $20 to Trinidad)
    • Free shipping: For orders over $100
    • Local pickup: Customer collects from you
  3. Test shipping calculations with different products
📦 Caribbean shipping resources:
• Caribshopper – Consolidates shipments to USA/UK
• DHL/FedEx – Reliable but expensive
• Local couriers – Often cheaper for local delivery
• TTPOST (Trinidad), Jamaica Post, etc. – National postal services

🔧 Troubleshooting Common Caribbean E-commerce Problems

❌ "Stripe isn't available in my country"
✅ Use First Atlantic Commerce, Plug n Pay, or PayPal instead. Or consider a US LLC (Option 3) to access Stripe.
❌ "Shopify Payments won't activate"
✅ Install a third-party gateway plugin like "PayPal" or "Authorize.net" (if you have a US bank account).
❌ "Bank says I need a merchant account – it takes weeks"
✅ Start with PayPal immediately while you wait. You can add the merchant account later.
❌ "Shipping costs are too high for customers"
✅ Offer free shipping over $X, or use services like Caribshopper for international orders.
❌ "Customers don't trust online payments"
✅ Add trust badges, display contact info, offer cash on delivery for first orders, share customer reviews.
❌ "My site is slow on mobile"
✅ Compress images (TinyPNG), use a caching plugin (W3 Total Cache), upgrade hosting if needed.
❌ "I have a US LLC but can't open a bank account"
✅ Use Mercury, Relay, or Wise for remote onboarding. Have your bank-ready packet ready with stamped Articles and Operating Agreement.
❌ "I didn't know about Form 5472"
⚠️ File immediately. The penalty is $25,000. Work with a US tax professional to correct.

🇺🇸 US LLC: Complete Setup Guide

A

Step 1: Choose Your State

🏔️ Wyoming

Best for: Privacy, low costs, online businesses
  • ✓ No state income tax
  • ✓ Low startup fees (~$100)
  • ✓ Strong privacy (no public member disclosure)
  • ✓ Minimum annual fees (~$60)

⚖️ Delaware

Best for: Venture capital, future investors, legal predictability
  • ✓ Respected Court of Chancery
  • ✓ Strong investor preference
  • ✓ No public member disclosure
  • ⚠️ Higher annual franchise tax ($300)

☀️ Florida

Best for: Businesses with US operations or customers
  • ✓ No personal state income tax
  • ✓ Large consumer base
  • ⚠️ Annual report fee $138.75
  • ⚠️ Requires in-state presence
💡 Recommendation: Start with Wyoming for low costs and privacy. Switch to Delaware later if seeking venture capital.
B

Step 2: Appoint a Registered Agent

Every US LLC requires a Registered Agent with a physical address in your formation state. They receive legal documents on your behalf. Costs $50-300/year .

💡 Popular agents: Northwest Registered Agent, ZenBusiness, LegalZoom
C

Step 3: File Articles of Organization

Submit to the Secretary of State with filing fee ($90-500 depending on state). This legally creates your LLC .

📋 Required info: Company name, registered agent, purpose, member names (varies by state)
D

Step 4: Get Your EIN (Employer Identification Number)

Apply to the IRS for your federal tax ID. Required for bank accounts and tax filings. As a non-resident, use Form SS-4 by fax or mail. Processing takes 4-8 weeks .

⚠️ Critical: The online EIN application requires an SSN or ITIN. Non-residents MUST use fax or mail .
E

Step 5: Draft an Operating Agreement

Even if not required by your state, create one. It establishes ownership, management, and profit distribution. Essential for banking .

F

Step 6: Open a US Bank Account

You'll need a "bank-ready packet" including :

  • Stamped Articles of Organization
  • EIN confirmation letter
  • Operating agreement naming manager and owners
  • Passport copies of all beneficial owners
  • Proof of address (utility bill, bank statement)
  • Short business plan explaining payment flows
💡 Online banks for non-residents: Mercury, Relay, Wise support remote onboarding
G

Step 7: Comply with US Tax Requirements

Foreign-owned disregarded entities must file Form 5472 for reportable transactions. Missing this triggers a $25,000 penalty .

  • ✓ File Form 5472 + pro-forma Form 1120 annually
  • ✓ BOI reporting may apply (check FinCEN requirements)
  • ✓ No US tax if no US-sourced income and no US trade or business
H

Step 8: Set Up Payment Processing

With your US LLC and bank account, you can now access :

  • ✅ Stripe (full access)
  • ✅ Shopify Payments
  • ✅ PayPal (US account)
  • ✅ Square
🚨 COST COMPARISON: DIY formations often fail bank KYC and can cost $87,000+ in penalties vs $4,500 for professional formation . Work with experts.
